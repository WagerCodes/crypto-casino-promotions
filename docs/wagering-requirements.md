# Wagering Requirements Explained

Wagering requirements describe how much activity is required before certain bonuses or rewards can be withdrawn or fully unlocked.

This document explains wagering requirements in plain language.

---

## What wagering requirements are

A wagering requirement is a multiplier applied to a bonus or reward.

For example:
- A bonus of 10 units with a 20Ã— requirement may require 200 units of wagering before withdrawal eligibility.

Requirements vary widely between platforms and promotions.

---

## Where wagering requirements apply

Wagering requirements may apply to:
- Deposit match bonuses
- Free spins or free cases
- Cashback or rakeback systems
- Tiered or loyalty rewards

Not all promotions include wagering requirements.

---

## Common wagering structures

Typical structures include:
- Bonus-only wagering (only the bonus amount is wagered)
- Deposit + bonus wagering
- Fixed wagering caps
- Game-specific contribution rates

Some games may contribute less toward wagering completion.

---

## Why wagering requirements exist

Platforms use wagering requirements to:
- Prevent abuse of promotions
- Encourage engagement with platform features
- Manage promotional risk

Understanding requirements helps users decide whether a promotion suits their preferences.

---

## What users should check

Before using a promotion, users should review:
- the wagering multiplier
- eligible games or activities
- maximum withdrawal limits
- time limits for completion
- restrictions or exclusions

Missing these details can lead to confusion.

---

## No guarantees

Meeting wagering requirements does not guarantee profit or success.

Promotions are optional and should be approached carefully.

---

## Responsible use

Users should:
- set clear limits
- understand terms before participating
- avoid chasing requirements

**18+ only. Gamble responsibly.**
